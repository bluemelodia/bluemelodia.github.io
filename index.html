One fine, relatively pollen-free spring afternoon in Baltimore, Dr. Charlene Gamaldo and I were sitting in her office at the Johns Hopkins hospital, reviewing the storyboard that I'd helped design for one of her online sleep courses. Once finalized, we would send the storyboard to a team of programmers, who would then build and ship the module for us. Although I had only a vague idea of what programmers did back then, I couldn't help but envy their ability to contribute significantly to the app development process. That was back in May 2013. I was a Behavioral Biology major and ex-premed, unsure of my future but certain that I wanted to do something other than research for a living.
Four months later, on September 3, 2013, I wrote my first line of code.
On September 3, 2014, my first iPhone game, Sleepy Head: Survive to Sunrise, became available for download on the App Store.
