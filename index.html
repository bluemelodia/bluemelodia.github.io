One fine, relatively pollen-free spring afternoon in Baltimore, Dr. Charlene Gamaldo and I were sitting in her office at the Johns Hopkins hospital, reviewing the storyboard that I'd helped design for one of her online sleep courses. Once finalized, we would be sending the storyboard to a team of programmers, who would then build the app for us. Although I had only the vaguest idea of what programmers did back then, I couldn't help but a little jealous at their ability to contribute significantly to the app-developing process. That was back in Spring 2013. I was a Behavioral Biology major and ex-premed, unsure of my future but pretty sure that I did not want to continue down my current path. Never could I have imagined that I would write my first line of code half a year later.
