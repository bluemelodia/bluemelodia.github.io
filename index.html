One fine, relatively pollen-free spring afternoon in Baltimore, Dr. Charlene Gamaldo and I were sitting in her office at the Johns Hopkins hospital, reviewing the storyboard that I'd helped design for one of her online sleep courses. Once finalized, we would be sending the storyboard to a team of programmers, who would then build the app for us. Although I had next to no idea what programmers did back then, I couldn't help but feel a tinge of jealousy at the fact that they got to do the bulk of the app-development.
